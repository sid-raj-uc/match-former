Key findings:
1. On hard pairs, vanilla AUC is literally 0% — the model fails completely.
2. Epipolar constraint rescues 39% of hard pairs (127/323).
3. When epipolar hurts, it's because the mask kills too many matches (E_n → 0).
   A fallback (use vanilla when epipolar yields <5 matches) would fix most degradations.
4. The biggest improvements are 70-80° reductions in pose error.
5. Epipolar benefit is invisible in overall AUC (easy pairs dominate the average)
   but critical for hard wide-baseline pairs.
